Mediterranean Chicken

Description

Made in just one skillet, this Mediterranean chicken recipe comes together in less than 30-minutes. It’s packed with my favorite flavors from childhood: garlicky seared chicken, briny olives, creamy feta, tomatoes, and parsley. Fewer dishes, less time, and more flavor—that’s what I call weeknight dinner done right!

Ingredients

  • 4 Boneless,skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons miced garlic or garlic paste
  • Kosher Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • 1 tablespoon drieg oregano, divided
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup dry white whine
  • 1 large lemon,juiced
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 small tomatoes, diced, about 1 1/2 cups
  • 1/4 cup sliced green olives
  • fresh parsley stems removed
  • crumbled feta cheese

Instructions

  1. Prep the chicken: Pat the chicken breasts dry. Make three shallow slits on each side of the chicken breast.
  2. Season the chicken: Rub the garlic on both sides of the chicken, pushing some garlic into the slits you made. Season the chicken breasts on both sides with salt, pepper and ½ of the dried oregano. Sear the chicken: In a large cast iron skillet, heat the olive oil
  3. Sear the chicken: In a large cast iron skillet, heat the olive oil on medium-high. Sear the chicken on both sides, then add the white wine and reduce by half.
  4. Braise the chicken: Add the lemon juice and chicken broth. Sprinkle the remaining ½ tablespoon of oregano on top and turn the heat to medium. Cover with a lid or tightly with foil. Cook for about 5 to 6 minutes on one side, then turn the chicken over and cook for 5 to 6 additional minutes, or until the internal temperature of the chicken reaches 165°F.
  5. Finish the chicken and serve: Uncover and top with chopped onions, tomatoes and olives. Cover again and cook for 3 minutes. Finish with the parsley and feta cheese (if using). Enjoy!
